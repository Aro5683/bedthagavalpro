
 *NGOs Working for Child Rights*
Children are the most important part of our world's future. However, not all children get equal opportunities or a safe environment. While the government creates many laws, *NGOs (Non-Governmental Organizations)* act as the bridge that ensures these benefits actually reach children in every corner of society.
*What is an NGO?*
An NGO is a voluntary organization that functions independently of the government for social welfare. It is a *non-profit* entity.
*Their Role in Tamil Nadu:*
In Tamil Nadu, these organizations excel in various fields, such as eradicating child labor, providing quality education, and rehabilitating children with disabilities.
Essentially, if a child is working instead of going to school, or if a child lacks necessary medical facilities, these organizations step in directly to restore their rights.
*12 Important NGOs for Children in Tamil Nadu*
*1. CCRD (Centre for Child Rights and Development)*
 *Established:* 1988 in Chennai.
*What they do:* They act as a *'Resource Centre.'* They provide training to smaller NGOs and offer consultations and advice to the government.
 *Example:* If a new group starts fighting for child rights in a village, CCRD will teach and guide them on how to operate.
*2. Hand in Hand*
 *Location:* Kanchipuram.
 *What they do:* Their main goal is to **eliminate child labor.* They also provide shelter and education for underprivileged women and children.
 *Example:* If a young boy is found working in a tea shop or firecracker factory, they rescue him and enroll him in school.
*3. Eureka Child*
*What they do:* They provide education for rural children. Beyond teaching, they provide books, toys, stationery, and food.
 *Example:* Making subjects like Tamil, Maths, and Science fun and easy to understand for a boy in a remote village, while providing all his school supplies.
*4. Sri Sakthi Trust*
 *Established:* 2001 in Dindigul.
 *What they do:* They create awareness about *'Sanitation'* in rural areas. They teach the importance of handwashing, bathing, and keeping the environment clean.
 *Example:* Visiting villages to campaign for cleanliness and encouraging parents to keep their children in school.
*5. Kaakkum Karangal (Guiding Hands)*
 *Established:* 1992 in Chennai.
 *What they do:* It serves as a large home for orphaned children on the streets and abandoned elderly people. They provide food, shelter, medical care, and education.
 *Example:* Bringing in a homeless child, providing them with care and clothes, and paving the way for a better life.
*6. Bhumi*
 *Network:* Driven by thousands of young, energetic *volunteers.*
 *What they do:* They provide education to orphaned and underprivileged children in backward areas.
 *Example:* A college student spending their weekends teaching English to children in an orphanage.
*7. Mithra*
 *Established:* 1977 in Chennai.
 *What they do:* They work specifically for *children with disabilities** (both physical and mental). They provide free education up to age 16.
 *Example:* Giving personalized attention to children who have difficulty walking or speaking to help them progress in life.
*8. Agaram Foundation*
 *Founder:* Founded by actor *Suriya* in 2006.
 *What they do:* They pay college fees for students who excel in studies but lack financial means. They also provide personality development classes.
 *Example:* Adopting a girl who scored high marks in the 12th grade but cannot afford college, and supporting her until she becomes a professional.
*9. Save the Children*
*Scope:* A global organization based in London.
 *What they do:* They assist in children's health, safety, and education. They are especially active during *disasters* (like tsunamis or floods).
*Example:* If a cyclone destroys an area, they immediately provide medical check-ups and food for the affected children.
*10. Amar Seva Sangam*
 *Location:* Ayikudi Village, Tenkasi (formerly Tirunelveli).
*What they do:* They work for people and children with disabilities in rural areas, planning with the community to improve their lives.
 *Example:* Providing mobility aids (like wheelchairs) and vocational training to a person with a physical disability so they can live independently.
 *11. EKAM Foundation*
 *Mission:* Their policy is *"Every child deserves healthcare."* They provide medical aid to newborns and pregnant women from underprivileged families.
 *Example:* Funding a life-saving surgery for a baby born into a family that cannot afford the medical costs.
*12. Annai Charitable & Educational Trust**
 *Founders:* Joseph Xavier and Amala Naayagi.
*What they do:* They serve the oppressed, the poor, women, and persons with disabilities.
 *Example:* Helping children from the most socially backward families get an education and establishing a stable livelihood for them.
