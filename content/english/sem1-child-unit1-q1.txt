<h2>NGOs Working for Child Rights</h2>
<p>
Children are the most important part of our world's future. However, not all children get equal opportunities or a safe environment. While the government creates many laws, NGOs (Non-Governmental Organizations) act as the bridge that ensures these benefits actually reach children in every corner of society.
</p>

<h3>What is an NGO?</h3>
<p>
An NGO is a voluntary organization that functions independently of the government for social welfare. It is a non-profit entity.
</p>

<h3>Their Role in Tamil Nadu:</h3>
<p>
In Tamil Nadu, these organizations excel in various fields, such as eradicating child labor, providing quality education, and rehabilitating children with disabilities.
</p>
<p>
Essentially, if a child is working instead of going to school, or if a child lacks necessary medical facilities, these organizations step in directly to restore their rights.
</p>

<h3>12 Important NGOs for Children in Tamil Nadu</h3>

<h4>1. CCRD (Centre for Child Rights and Development)</h4>
<p><b>Established:</b> 1988 in Chennai.</p>
<p><b>What they do:</b> They act as a 'Resource Centre.' They provide training to smaller NGOs and offer consultations and advice to the government.</p>
<p><b>Example:</b> If a new group starts fighting for child rights in a village, CCRD will teach and guide them on how to operate.</p>

<h4>2. Hand in Hand</h4>
<p><b>Location:</b> Kanchipuram.</p>
<p><b>What they do:</b> Their main goal is to eliminate child labor. They also provide shelter and education for underprivileged women and children.</p>
<p><b>Example:</b> If a young boy is found working in a tea shop or firecracker factory, they rescue him and enroll him in school.</p>

<h4>3. Eureka Child</h4>
<p><b>What they do:</b> They provide education for rural children. Beyond teaching, they provide books, toys, stationery, and food.</p>
<p><b>Example:</b> Making subjects like Tamil, Maths, and Science fun and easy to understand for a boy in a remote village, while providing all his school supplies.</p>

<h4>4. Sri Sakthi Trust</h4>
<p><b>Established:</b> 2001 in Dindigul.</p>
<p><b>What they do:</b> They create awareness about sanitation in rural areas. They teach the importance of hygiene and cleanliness.</p>
<p><b>Example:</b> Visiting villages to campaign for cleanliness and encouraging parents to keep their children in school.</p>

<h4>5. Kaakkum Karangal (Guiding Hands)</h4>
<p><b>Established:</b> 1992 in Chennai.</p>
<p><b>What they do:</b> It serves as a large home for orphaned children and abandoned elderly people. They provide food, shelter, medical care, and education.</p>
<p><b>Example:</b> Bringing in a homeless child, providing them with care and clothes, and paving the way for a better life.</p>

<h4>6. Bhumi</h4>
<p><b>Network:</b> Driven by thousands of young volunteers.</p>
<p><b>What they do:</b> They provide education to orphaned and underprivileged children in backward areas.</p>
<p><b>Example:</b> A college student spending weekends teaching English to children in an orphanage.</p>

<h4>7. Mithra</h4>
<p><b>Established:</b> 1977 in Chennai.</p>
<p><b>What they do:</b> They work for children with disabilities. They provide free education up to age 16.</p>
<p><b>Example:</b> Giving personalized attention to children with physical or speech difficulties.</p>

<h4>8. Agaram Foundation</h4>
<p><b>Founder:</b> Actor Suriya (2006).</p>
<p><b>What they do:</b> They fund higher education for deserving students and provide personality development.</p>
<p><b>Example:</b> Supporting a high-scoring student who cannot afford college.</p>

<h4>9. Save the Children</h4>
<p><b>Scope:</b> Global organization based in London.</p>
<p><b>What they do:</b> Focus on children's health, safety, and education, especially during disasters.</p>
<p><b>Example:</b> Providing relief and medical aid after natural disasters.</p>

<h4>10. Amar Seva Sangam</h4>
<p><b>Location:</b> Ayikudi, Tenkasi.</p>
<p><b>What they do:</b> Support children and people with disabilities in rural areas.</p>
<p><b>Example:</b> Providing wheelchairs and vocational training.</p>

<h4>11. EKAM Foundation</h4>
<p><b>Mission:</b> Every child deserves healthcare.</p>
<p><b>What they do:</b> Provide medical aid to newborns and pregnant women.</p>
<p><b>Example:</b> Funding life-saving surgeries for babies.</p>

<h4>12. Annai Charitable & Educational Trust</h4>
<p><b>Founders:</b> Joseph Xavier and Amala Naayagi.</p>
<p><b>What they do:</b> Support poor communities, women, and children with education and livelihood.</p>
<p><b>Example:</b> Helping underprivileged children get education and stability.</p>
