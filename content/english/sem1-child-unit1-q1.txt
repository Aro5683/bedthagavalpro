# NGOs Working for Child Rights
Children are the most important part of our world's future. However, not all children get equal opportunities or a safe environment. While the government creates many laws, NGOs (Non-Governmental Organizations) act as the bridge that ensures these benefits actually reach children in every corner of society.

## What is an NGO?
An NGO is a voluntary organization that functions independently of the government for social welfare. It is a non-profit entity.

## Their Role in Tamil Nadu:
In Tamil Nadu, these organizations excel in various fields, such as eradicating child labor, providing quality education, and rehabilitating children with disabilities.
Essentially, if a child is working instead of going to school, or if a child lacks necessary medical facilities, these organizations step in directly to restore their rights.

## 12 Important NGOs for Children in Tamil Nadu

## 1. CCRD (Centre for Child Rights and Development)
**Established:** 1988 in Chennai  
**What they do:** They act as a Resource Centre. They provide training to smaller NGOs and offer consultations and advice to the government.  
> Example: If a new group starts fighting for child rights in a village, CCRD will teach and guide them on how to operate.

## 2. Hand in Hand
**Location:** Kanchipuram  
**What they do:** Their main goal is to eliminate child labor. They also provide shelter and education for underprivileged women and children.  
> Example: If a young boy is found working in a tea shop or factory, they rescue him and enroll him in school.

## 3. Eureka Child
**What they do:** They provide education for rural children along with books, toys, stationery, and food.  
> Example: Making subjects like Tamil, Maths, and Science easy and fun for rural students.

## 4. Sri Sakthi Trust
**Established:** 2001 in Dindigul  
**What they do:** They create awareness about sanitation and hygiene in rural areas.  
> Example: Campaigning for cleanliness and encouraging school attendance.

## 5. Kaakkum Karangal (Guiding Hands)
**Established:** 1992 in Chennai  
**What they do:** Provides shelter, food, education, and medical care for orphaned children and the elderly.  
> Example: Helping homeless children rebuild their lives.

## 6. Bhumi
**Network:** Volunteer-driven organization  
**What they do:** Provides education to underprivileged children.  
> Example: College students teaching children in orphanages.

## 7. Mithra
**Established:** 1977 in Chennai  
**What they do:** Supports children with disabilities with free education.  
> Example: Personalized care for children with physical or speech challenges.

## 8. Agaram Foundation
**Founder:** Actor Suriya (2006)  
**What they do:** Funds higher education and provides skill development.  
> Example: Supporting students who cannot afford college.

## 9. Save the Children
**Scope:** Global organization  
**What they do:** Works on child health, safety, and education, especially during disasters.  
> Example: Providing relief during floods or cyclones.

## 10. Amar Seva Sangam
**Location:** Ayikudi, Tenkasi  
**What they do:** Supports children and people with disabilities in rural areas.  
> Example: Providing wheelchairs and vocational training.

## 11. EKAM Foundation
**Mission:** Every child deserves healthcare  
**What they do:** Provides medical aid to newborns and mothers.  
> Example: Funding life-saving surgeries.

## 12. Annai Charitable & Educational Trust
**Founders:** Joseph Xavier and Amala Naayagi  
**What they do:** Supports poor communities, women, and children through education and livelihood programs.  
> Example: Helping underprivileged children build stable futures.
