# 🌀 Millennium Development Goals (MDGs) - A Simple Explanation

Before the SDGs (2015–2030) that we already looked at, all the countries in the world came together in the year 2000 and created a plan called the MDGs. This plan was in effect until 2015.  
You can simply call these the "8 Super Goals."

## ⚡ The 8 Goals (With Real-Life Examples)

The MDGs consist of 8 main goals and 21 specific targets. Here are the key points from your book explained simply:

## ⚜️ MDG 1: Eradicate Extreme Poverty and Hunger

The aim was to halve the number of people earning less than $1.25 a day (about ₹100).

> Example:  
Implementing schemes like the MGNREGA (100-day work scheme) to ensure a steady income for poor families.

## ⚜️ MDG 2: Achieve Universal Primary Education

By 2015, every boy and girl in the world should have completed a full course of Primary Education.

> Example:  
Bringing rural children to school through programs like SSA (Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan) and providing free mid-day meals to keep them studying.

## ⚜️ MDG 3: Promote Gender Equality and Empower Women

Eliminate the disparity between boys and girls at all levels of education, from primary school to university.

> Example:  
Providing special scholarships for girls in college to help them become engineers, doctors, and professionals.

## ⚜️ MDG 4 & 5: Reduce Child Mortality and Improve Maternal Health

Reduce the death rate of children under 5 by two-thirds, and reduce the maternal mortality rate (deaths during childbirth) by 75%.

> Example:  
Providing Nutrition Kits to pregnant women and ensuring that all births take place safely in hospitals.

## ⚜️ MDG 6: Combat HIV/AIDS, Malaria, and Other Diseases

Stop the spread of diseases like HIV/AIDS and Malaria and start reversing the trend.

> Example:  
Cleaning drains to prevent Malaria spread by mosquitoes and providing preventive medicines.

## ⚜️ MDG 7: Ensure Environmental Sustainability

Stop the destruction of nature and improve the lives of at least 100 million slum dwellers by 2020.

> Example:  
Building permanent houses for people living in slums and providing them with clean tap water facilities.

## ⚜️ MDG 8: Develop a Global Partnership for Development

Solve the debt problems of poor countries and ensure that internet access and medicines are available at affordable prices.

> Example:  
Big countries sharing their vaccines or new technology with smaller countries to help them progress.

## 📍 Important Note:

There is a key point in your book: " _The MDGs have been superseded by Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)._ "  
This means that after the MDGs ended in 2015, they were expanded and transformed into the 17 SDGs we see today.

## Exam Tips:

When writing your exam, make sure to mention:

> " _The MDGs started in 2000 and ended in 2015; the SDGs followed them to continue the work_ ."
