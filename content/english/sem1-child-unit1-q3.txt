# 🪻 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)

Think of this simply as **"A SUPER PLAN for the World."** In 2015, all the countries in the world came together and decided on 17 goals to answer the question: **"How should we change our world by 2030?"**

## This plan is often called the 5 Ps:

## 1. People:
There should be no poverty.

## 2. Planet:
We must protect nature.

## 3. Prosperity:
Everyone should live a comfortable life.

## 4. Peace:
There should be no fights or conflicts.

## 5. Partnership:
All countries must work together.

## 🌀 The 17 Goals (Key Points & Examples)
When writing your exam, remember them like this:

## 1. No Poverty:
No one should suffer because of a lack of money.

> Example:
Everyone in a village should have a job and their own home.

## 2. Zero Hunger:
No one should go to sleep without even a single meal.

> Example:
Helping farmers so that everyone gets nutritious food.

## 3. Good Health and Well-being:
Everyone should be healthy and free from disease.

> Example:
Good hospital facilities even in villages and ensuring children get vaccinated.

## 4. Quality Education:
This is most important for you! Every child should have the chance to go to school.

> Example:
Providing laptops and quality teaching even to children who cannot afford it.

## 5. Gender Equality:
Girls should have the same rights as boys.

> Example:
Just as men go to work, women should also be able to work and prove their talents.

## 6. Clean Water and Sanitation:
Everyone should have access to clean water for drinking and bathing.

> Example:
Preventing river pollution and ensuring every house gets clean water.

## 7. Affordable and Clean Energy:
Everyone should have electricity, produced in a way that doesn't destroy nature.

> Example:
Using "Solar Panels" on roofs to get electricity from sunlight so the air stays clean.

## 8. Decent Work and Economic Growth:
Everyone should earn a living through dignified work without suffering.

> Example:
Paying employees fair wages and not treating them like slaves. Starting new businesses to create jobs.

## 9. Industry, Innovation, and Infrastructure:
Towns should have good roads, bridges, and internet. We must invent new things.

> Example:
Bringing high-speed internet to villages so a boy in a rural area can learn anything in the world.

## 10. Reduced Inequalities:
There should be no discrimination based on rich-poor, caste, or religion.

> Example:
A rich child and a poor child sitting on the same bench in school. No special privileges for anyone.

## 11. Sustainable Cities and Communities:
The places where we live must be safe and clean.

> Example:
Planting more trees and managing waste properly instead of throwing it on the streets.

## 12. Responsible Consumption and Production:
Do not waste anything. Use only what is necessary.

> Example:
Not wasting food at weddings. Avoiding plastic bags and using cloth bags instead.

## 13. Climate Action:
We must stop the Earth from getting too hot (Global Warming).

> Example:
Growing more plants and trees. Avoiding smoke-emitting vehicles and using cycles or electric vehicles.

## 14. Life Below Water:
We must protect the fish and creatures living in oceans and rivers.

> Example:
Not dumping plastic waste into the ocean to save fish and marine resources.

## 15. Life on Land:
We must protect forests and not hunt animals.

> Example:
Not destroying forests to build apartments. Protecting species like sparrows and peacocks from extinction.

## 16. Peace, Justice, and Strong Institutions:
Everyone should live peacefully without conflict. The law should be equal for all.

> Example:
Getting a fair judgment from the police or court for any problem. There should be no corruption.

## 17. Partnerships for the Goals:
To achieve the 16 points above, all countries must unite.

> Example:
One country sharing its new technology with another so everyone can progress together.

## Child Rights & SDGs

This is a very important part of your book. "Why should we focus on children in these 17 goals?"

## Leaving No Child Behind:
There is a rule in this plan that "No one should be left out." Specifically, children in need (poor children, children with disabilities) must be brought to school.

## Children as Agents of Change:
This plan says children shouldn't just be students; they should be the heroes who change the world.

## A Healthy Future:
If we take care of the Earth today, you will have clean air and water when you grow up.

## The Big Idea (Summary):

The main point of these SDGs is:

> **"We must not destroy nature just for us to live today. We must leave the Earth safely for the children of tomorrow (Future Generations)."**
