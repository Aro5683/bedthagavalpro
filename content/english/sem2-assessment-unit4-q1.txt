## How to Construct a Diagnostic Test

In simple terms, just like a doctor uses a blood test to identify a patient’s illness, a **diagnostic test** is used to find out exactly where a student is facing difficulty in a subject.

## Steps in Constructing a Diagnostic Test

### 1. Planning

First, decide **for whom the test is intended**.

Usually, these tests are given to students who score very low marks in regular tests (achievement tests)—often the **bottom 10%**, referred to as *“backward in learning.”*

**Example:**  
If more than half of the class makes mistakes in converting **Centigrade to Fahrenheit**, it means there is a problem in that specific concept.


### 2. Writing the Test Items

This is the most important step.

Break a big topic into **small learning points**.

**Example (Thermometer topic):**
- Do students know the **Lower Fixed Point**?
- Do they know the **Upper Fixed Point**?
- Do they know the formula:

F = (C × 9/5) + 32

Ask only:
- Very short answer questions  
- Objective-type questions (MCQs)

❌ Avoid long essay-type questions.


### 3. Assembling the Question Paper

Arrange questions in a proper sequence:
- Start from **easy (known)**  
- Move towards **difficult (unknown)**  


### 4. Giving Clear Instructions

Students should not feel afraid.

Make it clear:
- This is **not for grading or failing**
- It is only to understand **what they have not learned**

Allow them to:
- Write **slowly and comfortably**
- Not worry too much about time limits


### 5. Scoring Key & Marking Scheme

Give marks for **each small step**.

This helps the teacher identify:
- Whether the student made a mistake in the **formula**
- Or in **calculation (addition/subtraction, etc.)**


## Diagnosis and Remedial Measures

After the test, prepare a **Diagnostic Table**:

| Student Name | Q1 | Q2 | ... | Q15 | Q16 |
|--------------|----|----|-----|-----|-----|
| Student A    | ✅ | ✅ |     | ❌  | ❌  |

From this table, you can easily identify:
- Which questions most students got wrong

**Example Insight:**  
If many students got Questions 15 and 16 wrong, the teacher may conclude:

> Students do not understand the **BODMAS rule**, which is causing calculation errors.


## Remedial Measures (Solutions)

Now that the problem is identified:

❌ Do NOT reteach the entire lesson from the beginning  

✔ Instead:
- Reteach only the **problem area**
- Give more **examples and practice**
- Use a **different teaching method** if needed


## Important Concept: Item Analysis

A good test should not be:
- Too easy ❌  
- Too difficult ❌  

This is checked using **Difficulty Level (DL)**.

- Ideal value: **0.50 (moderate difficulty)**
- Very low value → Question is too difficult → should be removed


## In Short

- A **Diagnostic Test** is like using a microscope to find exactly where a student struggles  
- **Remedial Teaching** is helping the student exactly at that weak point  


💡 **Tip for B.Ed Exams:**
Drawing **mind maps** and **flowcharts** can help you score better marks and present your answer clearly.
